# The company

**Huggabowl** is a Calgary-based registered Canadian charity that serves
hot, nourishing meals from a food cart at **Olympic Plaza** in downtown
Calgary.

- **Charitable status:** CRA Charitable Compliant — registration number
  `735654550RR0001`. Donations are eligible for Canadian charitable tax
  receipts.
- **Location:** Olympic Plaza, 228 8 Ave SE, Calgary, Alberta. Service is
  seasonal/weather-dependent — follow social media for the next pop-up.
- **Founded:** Calgary-grown grassroots project; covered in early days by
  CBC News, later by CTV News and CityNews as the model scaled.

## What it actually does

Three things, end-to-end:

1. **Rescues surplus food** — partners with local restaurants, suppliers,
   and producers to take in food that would otherwise be discarded.
2. **Transforms it into real meals** — volunteer chefs cook the rescued
   ingredients into high-quality soups and other hot meals in a shared
   kitchen.
3. **Serves it from a street cart** — the cart parks at Olympic Plaza;
   customers buy a bowl (and effectively, a second bowl); tokens are
   handed to neighbours facing food insecurity so they can come back any
   time and redeem one with no questions asked.

The result: food waste goes down, hungry people get fed with dignity, and
the people who pay for a bowl get a great meal _and_ know they fed
someone else.

## Voice and brand notes

A few snippets the existing site has used and that we should preserve in
the new site's copy:

- _"Buy a meal, give a meal — made from rescued food, served with purpose."_
  (tagline)
- _"Huggabowls are hot, nourishing meals served from our food truck — and
  when you buy a $5 token, we pass that same meal on to someone facing
  food insecurity, no questions asked."_ (token mechanic)
- _"Everyone deserves a nourishing meal — our Huggabowls are served with
  dignity, whether you're buying lunch or using a token."_ (dignity
  principle)
- _"We purchase surplus food from local businesses and turn it into fresh,
  delicious meals instead of letting it go to waste."_ (food rescue)

Keep the tone warm, plainspoken, and proud — never pitying toward
recipients, never preachy toward donors.
