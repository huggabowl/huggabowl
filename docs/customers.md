# Who uses Huggabowl

The site speaks to **five distinct audiences**. Knowing which page is
aimed at which audience helps us write copy and prioritize features.

## 1. Lunch buyers (the "paying customer")

Who: people working/visiting downtown Calgary, walking past Olympic
Plaza. They want a hot, real lunch with a story behind it.

What they need from the site:

- Where the cart is (`/visit-us`) and roughly when it's open.
- What's on the menu (today / typically) — currently expressed
  generically as "rotating soups."
- Confidence the place is legit — the press coverage on `/stories` does
  most of this work.

Lunch buyers don't usually convert online; they convert at the cart.

## 2. Donors (online conversion)

Who: people who care about food security in Calgary, who follow Huggabowl
on social, or who can't physically visit the cart. The donate flow is
their primary path.

What they need from the site:

- A clear "Donate" CTA from every page (Nav has one).
- A friendly donate form with sensible default amounts and a custom
  field (`/donate`).
- A trustworthy redirect to Stripe Checkout.
- A receipt + a clear note about CRA tax receipts.
- A thank-you page that reinforces the impact (`/donate/success`).

This is the **most important conversion path** on the site.

## 3. Volunteers

Who: home cooks, retired chefs, students looking for community service,
people with food handling certificates wanting to give back. Also:
drivers willing to pick up rescued food.

What they need from the site:

- A clear overview of roles (`/volunteer`).
- A way to express interest — currently a `volunteer@huggabowl.com`
  email. Could be upgraded to a form later.

## 4. Event organizers (catering customers)

Who: corporate offices, conference planners, community groups in
Calgary. They want lunch for an event and like the social mission.

What they need from the site:

- A short "yes we cater" page with what we serve and how to quote
  (`/catering`).
- A `catering@huggabowl.com` contact path.

Catering is an **important revenue stream** because each event sells many
bowls at once, which funds many give-away meals.

## 5. Recipients (people facing food insecurity)

Who: anyone hungry in downtown Calgary.

The site is **not their primary touchpoint** — they meet Huggabowl at the
cart, where tokens or walk-up service work no-questions-asked. But the
site still matters for them indirectly: if it accurately conveys dignity
and welcome, frontline workers and community orgs are more comfortable
referring people to it.

What this means for the site:

- Never frame recipients as a problem to be solved.
- The `/visit-us` page should be readable, plain-language, and not
  fortressed behind donor copy.

## Partners (a sixth, quieter audience)

Restaurants, suppliers, and producers who donate or discount surplus
food. They mostly come in through personal/founder relationships rather
than the website, but a public mention/thank-you page is something we
could add later (out of scope for v1).
